**The Tapestry of Time**  

Beneath the sky, so vast, so wide,  
Where whispers of the winds reside,  
A tale unfolds, both old and new,  
Of fleeting moments, and skies of blue.  

The rivers carve their ancient song,  
Through valleys deep, they rush along,  
Their waters speak of years gone by,  
Of laughter, tears, and questions: why?  

The mountains stand, both proud and tall,  
Witness to the rise and fall,  
Of kingdoms built, of dreams once sown,  
Of seeds that grew, of seeds unknown.  

The stars above, eternal flame,  
Each one a spark, each one a name,  
They watch the earth with patient gaze,  
Through endless nights and countless days.  

The moon, a lantern in the night,  
Bathes the world in silver light,  
It waxes full, then fades away,  
A mirror to life's fleeting play.  

The forests hum with life unseen,  
A symphony of vibrant green,  
The rustling leaves, the bird's sweet call,  
A gentle hymn that binds us all.  

The ocean roars, a mighty force,  
Its waves chart time's unyielding course,  
It holds the secrets of the deep,  
Where ancient dreams and shadows sleep.  

And we, mere mortals, passing through,  
With hearts that yearn, with skies to blue,  
We weave our threads into the loom,  
Of joy, of sorrow, of love, of gloom.  

Each step we take, each breath we draw,  
Each moment filled with silent awe,  
We etch our stories on the page,  
A fleeting mark, a fleeting age.  

For time, relentless, marches on,  
From dusk to dusk, from dawn to dawn,  
It spares no king, it spares no slave,  
It binds us all, from birth to grave.  

Yet in its flow, a gift we find,  
A fleeting spark, a fleeting mind,  
To love, to dream, to laugh, to cry,  
To live, to learn, to question why.  

So let us dance beneath the stars,  
And mend our wounds, and heal our scars,  
For though the night may seem so long,  
The dawn will come, with morning's song.  

And when our threads are finally spun,  
When all is said, when all is done,  
We'll leave behind a tapestry,  
Of all we were, of all we'll be.  

So raise your voice, and sing your rhyme,  
And cherish this: the gift of time,  
For though it fades, it leaves its trace,  
A fleeting, yet eternal grace.
